MY Biography

Liz is a visionary enterpreneur, social activist, and CEO of Wema BDO, an impactful NGO in Western Kenya.Driven by an unshakable belief in community transformation and justice, Liz committed herself to uplifting marginalized voices through advocacy, business, and bold action. Commited to education, women's empowerment, and climate action, Liz's leadership at Wema drives transformation through a multifaceted approach.

1. Women Empowerment: -The Wema Girls' Soccer Cup nurtures talents and imparts life skills to 300 girls in 14 villages. The goal is to foster independence, prevent early pregnancies and marriages, and encourage girls to stay dedicated to their education. -The Wema Girls' Hostel is a rescue center for over 350 underprivileged girls. This isn’t merely a safe haven, it’s also a transformative platform that breaks down barriers, fosters self-belief, and fuels aspirations. These young girls blossom into empowered women who will drive lasting change within themselves and their communities.

2. Empowering Young Men: The Breinin House is a Boys' Center that accommodates and supports 250 young boys and men, offering them education, career guidance, and holistic development.

3. Empowering Through Education: The Lotus of Highway Schools is a beacon of quality education, fostering academic excellence and personal growth for more than 600 children within the immediate community.

4. Wema Technical Training Institute: Elizabeth founded the Wema Institute to equip women and youth with essential skills like computer literacy, entrepreneurship, baking, accounts, masonry & more .These skills empower them to contribute to their families and uplift their communities.

5. Championing Climate Action: Elizabeth is a climate activist and has set up several initiatives to champion for environmental sustainability. The initiatives include the Green Schools Program, Chwele River Greening Initiative, and the School of Forest Farmers. Elizabeth's involvement goes beyond Wema. She's a Charter President of the Lions Club of Bungoma, a Chapter President of DNALife Organization Kenya, and previously served as the Global Ambassador of the Unilever Foundation. Her entrepreneurial spirit shines through as the founder and C.E.O of Stery Limited, which runs supermarket, wholesale and bakery businesses; a venture dedicated to creating meaningful employment opportunities for youth and women in her community. Her journey from Accountant at Unilever Kenya to the helm of Wema, an organization founded by her parents in 2008, exemplifies her drive to make a lasting impact. Join Liz on her mission for a brighter, empowered, and sustainable future. Connect with her to explore synergies and collaborate on this journey.

Through Wema, her NGO, and Stery Supermarket, she’s not just building institutions—she’s building futures. She also serves as a mentor and speaker on youth entrepreneurship and sustainability across Africa.

My Hobbies

I must confess—I am a workaholic. I’m the kind of person who feels guilty when I’m not working. Lol. That said, I do have other passions. I love to travel. Though I haven’t explored much yet, the little I’ve seen has confirmed one thing: I’m a traveler at heart. This year, I’m excited to embark on a journey to five different countries—just the beginning of my global adventures. I have a deep appreciation for poetry and anything sentimental. As a Scorpio, I’m naturally drawn to depth, emotion, and the kind of beauty that stirs the soul. I also have a strong love for well-crafted language—proper English makes me swoon. I’ll admit, broken English in texts is a bit of a pet peeve! Fashion is another love of mine. During my fourth year at university, I even enrolled in a fashion design course. It was such an enjoyable experience, though I didn’t get to complete my final exams because they clashed with my degree project and finals. Still, it’s something I hold dear. I also enjoy singing and dancing, and modelling.

MEET LIZ JUMAH

Meet Elizabeth Jumah—a living testament to resilience, reinvention, and the extraordinary power of rising from rock bottom. Have you ever tried everything, only for nothing to work out? Elizabeth knows that pain intimately. At just 21, she landed her first job at Unilever—an achievement many dream of. She quickly rose to become the Ambassador of the Unilever Foundation, a prestigious role that allowed her to travel and touch the lives of 2.5 million farmers in India. Her future sparkled with promise, and her life began to shift in exciting, bold directions. But just two years later, at only 23, her world came crashing down. She was retrenched. One day she had a flourishing career—and the next, she had nothing. It was one of her lowest, darkest moments. But Elizabeth made a vow: she would never go back to employment. Instead, she would pursue her dream—to become an entrepreneur. What followed was a painful yet powerful journey. She started over ten businesses: Uber services, car hire, fashion blogging, jewelry making, magazine publishing, a cleaning company, tenderpreneurship, agribusiness, snack distribution, bookselling—you name it. And one by one, they failed. By the end of three years, Elizabeth was drowning in debt. Her dreams had shattered. She was exhausted, broke, and broken. With nothing left, she returned home to Western Kenya—carrying nothing but hope and a determination to rebuild. And rebuild she did. Elizabeth founded Stery Supermarket and Bakery, which today employs scores of young men and women in her community. After three years of rebuilding, she asked herself a life-altering question: "How can I give back?" And that question became the seed of a revolution. Today, Elizabeth is the Executive Director of Wema Bukembe Development Organization, the Charter President of the Lions Club of Bungoma, the chapter president of DNALife Organization, Kenya, and the founder of multiple transformative projects that are changing the lives of over 5,000 people in her community. Her pain became her purpose. Her brokenness became the foundation for others to rise. Elizabeth Jumah is not just an entrepreneur. She is a community builder. A dream igniter. A social activist. A woman who turned failure into fuel—and is now lighting the way for others to find hope, dignity, and a second chance. In her presence, you’ll find more than inspiration—you’ll find a mentor, a compassionate ally, and a steadfast partner on the journey of transformation. With Elizabeth, you’ll encounter real stories, hard-earned wisdom, and practical tools for rising again. This is not just motivation—it’s a movement. Welcome to a journey of healing, discovery, and empowerment. Under Liz Jumah’s guidance, you’re not just surviving—you’re rewriting your story. Let’s begin, together.

ABOUT LIZ JUMAH

Liz is a visionary entrepreneur, social activist, and CEO of Wema BDO, an impactful NGO in Western Kenya. Committed to education, women's empowerment, and climate action, Liz's leadership at Wema drives transformations through a multifaceted approach:

1. Women Empowerment: -The Wema Girls' Soccer Cup nurtures talents and imparts life skills to 300 girls in 14 villages. The goal is to foster independence, prevent early pregnancies and marriages, and encourage girls to stay dedicated to their education. -The Wema Girls' Hostel is a rescue center for over 350 underprivileged girls. This isn’t merely a safe haven, it’s also a transformative platform that breaks down barriers, fosters self-belief, and fuels aspirations. These young girls blossom into empowered women who will drive lasting change within themselves and their communities.

2. Empowering Young Men: The Breinin House is a Boys' Center that accommodates and supports 250 young boys and men, offering them education, career guidance, and holistic development.

3. Empowering Through Education: The Lotus of Highway Schools is a beacon of quality education, fostering academic excellence and personal growth for more than 600 children within the immediate community.

4. Wema Technical Training Institute: Elizabeth founded the Wema Institute to equip women and youth with essential skills like computer literacy, entrepreneurship, baking, accounts, masonry & more .These skills empower them to contribute to their families and uplift their communities.

5. Championing Climate Action: Elizabeth is a climate activist and has set up several initiatives to champion for environmental sustainability. The initiatives include the Green Schools Program, Chwele River Greening Initiative, and the School of Forest Farmers. Elizabeth's involvement goes beyond Wema. She's a Charter President of the Lions Club of Bungoma, a Chapter President of DNALife Organization Kenya, and previously served as the Global Ambassador of the Unilever Foundation. Her entrepreneurial spirit shines through as the founder and C.E.O of Stery Limited, which runs supermarket, wholesale and bakery businesses; a venture dedicated to creating meaningful employment opportunities for youth and women in her community. Her journey from an Accountant at Unilever Kenya to the helm of Wema, an organization founded by her parents in 2008, exemplifies her drive to make a lasting impact. Join Liz on her mission for a brighter, empowered, and sustainable future. Connect with her at ejumah@wemabdo.org or visit www.wemabdo.org to support her service activities.

ABOUT LIZJUMAH

Liz Jumah is an internationally acclaimed entrepreneur, inspiring public speaker, and passionate mentor, known for her unwavering dedication to social impact and community empowerment. With a heart for mentoring youth and men, Liz uses her voice, vision, and experience to champion purpose-driven leadership and transformative change. She is the Founder and CEO of Stery Supermarket, a thriving business that has created employment opportunities for youth in Western Kenya. Her entrepreneurial journey—rooted in resilience and fueled by purpose—has inspired many to rise above setbacks and pursue their dreams. As the Executive Director of Wema Bukembe Development Organization (Wema BDO), Liz leads innovative programs in education, women empowerment, environmental conservation, and youth development. Her initiatives have positively impacted over 5,000 lives in her community and beyond. Liz is also the founder of several transformative programs and events, including: Wema Girls Cup – a soccer initiative empowering 350 girls through sports and life skills Wema Technical Training Institute – equipping young women with vocational and entrepreneurial skills Wema Women’s Conference – a platform connecting women and girls with African leaders to inspire and shape a better future In addition, Liz is the Charter President of the Lions Club of Bungoma, where she has successfully lobbied for critical development projects including water and sanitation systems that benefit thousands.
